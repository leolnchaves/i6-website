## Pain

Modern operations live with decisions that arrive late:

- **Rush orders** because no one saw demand rising.
- **Delivery delays** that turn into contractual penalties instead of avoided opportunities.
- **Reactive maintenance** when predictive signals were already flagging the asset weeks ago.
- **Teams operating dashboards**, not decisions — the data exists, the action does not.

## Problem

Traditional operations systems are **reactive by design**: they record what happened and alert when the threshold is breached. Three things are missing — and generic AI doesn't deliver them either:

1. **Probabilistic forecasting** that understands confidence intervals, not just points.
2. **Connection between forecast and action** — without activating the right team, a forecast becomes a chart.
3. **Contextual learning** — a model that understands seasonality, local constraints, real supplier lead time.

## Solution

infinity6 turns reactive operations into anticipatory operations:

- **i6 Previsio** — granular forecasting (SKU-store-day, asset-component-week) with full distribution and daily reassessment.
- **i6 RecSys** — prioritizes orders and allocations by expected margin and service-level impact, not by FIFO.
- **i6 Signal** — delivers the next action for purchasing, supply chain, logistics and maintenance, in business language.

## Application

The predictive layer runs on top of ERP, WMS, TMS and MES via API. Operations teams stay in current systems — they receive the prescriptive recommendation as input.

- **Causal alerts**: every alert ships with the factors that triggered it (explainability).
- **Financial-impact prioritization**: the queue is ordered by margin at risk, not arrival time.
- **Continuous learning loop**: every executed decision feeds the model.

- **−27%** Rush orders | Consumer goods manufacturing
- **+18%** OTIF (On-time, in-full) | Retail
- **−42%** Unplanned reactive maintenance | Industry

## FAQ

**Does it work for process industry, not just retail?**
Yes. i6 Previsio runs at SKU-store-day for retail and asset-component-week for industry, with the same foundation model and contextual fine-tuning.

**Do I need to integrate MES, WMS and TMS?**
Not in every case. We start with the highest-leverage system (usually ERP + WMS) and expand as ROI validates.

**How do operations teams adopt it?**
i6 Signal delivers the recommendation in business language (not JSON) — via Slack, email, embedded ERP panel or WhatsApp. The adoption curve is weeks, not months.

**Do the models handle shocks (strike, recall, climate event)?**
Yes. Daily reassessment + external memory (MAML) allow fast relearning on short windows; extreme events are flagged with widened confidence intervals.
