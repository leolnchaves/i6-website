## Pain

Most traffic doesn't convert for two simple reasons:

- The right offer arrives late — when the customer has already decided to leave.
- The argument is generic — "20% off" to someone who would have paid full price, "buy now" to someone still researching.

The cost is double: **inflated CAC** (spent to bring traffic that didn't convert) + **eroded margin** (unnecessary discount for those who would have bought anyway).

## Problem

Rule-based personalization ("if cart > X, show Y") treats all customers as equal within the segment. Generic propensity models use fragile signals and ignore **what makes that specific purchase happen now**: timing, context, similarity, elasticity.

Applied AI without explainability becomes a black box — the commercial team doesn't trust the recommendation and turns it off.

## Solution

infinity6 connects behavior, context and activation in three layers:

- **i6 RecSys** — purchase propensity score per SKU/channel/moment, including for anonymous traffic.
- **i6 ElasticPrice** — calibrates the right incentive (discount, bonus, bundle) by the real elasticity of the cluster.
- **i6 Signal** — delivers the dynamic argument (explainability becomes a ready phrase) to the activation channel: site, app, email, WhatsApp, store.

## Application

Plugs into e-commerce, CRM, app and POS via API. Every session event triggers propensity reassessment; the returning argument already comes with per-feature explanation.

- **Dynamic argument** — not "20% off"; it's "high engagement from similar profiles in the last 30 days" + "strong correlation with high-value items".
- **Native anonymization** — operates on anonymized identifiers.
- **Native A/B** — every recommendation carries a test window against baseline.

## FAQ

**Does it work for anonymous traffic (no login)?**
Yes. i6 RecSys generates propensity for anonymous visitors based on session pattern, device, context and similarity with known clusters.

**How does it avoid cannibalizing full-price sales?**
i6 ElasticPrice calibrates incentive by the real elasticity of the cluster — those who would buy without a discount get a value argument, not a coupon.

**Does the commercial team understand the recommendation?**
Yes. Explainability becomes a ready phrase: every recommendation returns the factors that support it (e.g. "high correlation with high-value customers", "category growth opportunity").

**How long until impact shows?**
On e-commerce with 30k+ sessions/day, the learning cycle calibrates in 3 to 4 weeks. Material results appear in the second month.
